Chapter 1:
The New City

Tom opens his eyes. He is in a new city. The city is big and very beautiful. There are many tall buildings and green trees. The sun is hot in the blue sky. Tom is happy but he is also a little tired.

He has a small blue suitcase in his hand. In the suitcase, there are three shirts, two pairs of pants, and a map. He looks at the map. "Where is my new house?" Tom asks himself. He walks down the street. The street is busy with people and cars.

Strona 2

Tom sees a small cafe. The cafe has small round tables outside. A woman is drinking coffee and reading a book. Tom walks to the cafe because he is thirsty. He wants a glass of cold water or a cup of hot tea.

A waiter comes to Tom. The waiter is a young man with a nice smile. "Hello," the waiter says. "Can I help you?" Tom smiles back. "Yes, please. Can I have a glass of water and a chicken sandwich?" Tom says. He sits down at a table.

Strona 3

The waiter brings the water and the sandwich. The sandwich is very good, and the water is cold. Tom eats slowly and looks at the people. A girl is walking with a big brown dog. The dog is very friendly. It wags its tail.

Tom takes his map again. He shows the map to the waiter. "Excuse me," Tom asks. "Where is Green Street?" The waiter looks at the map and points his finger. "Go straight, then turn left at the big supermarket. Green Street is there."

Strona 4

Chapter 2:
The Small Apartment

Tom thanks the waiter and pays for the food. He walks down the street. He sees the big supermarket. It has a lot of fruit and vegetables outside. Apples, bananas, and oranges look delicious. Tom turns left after the supermarket.

Now he is on Green Street. It is a quiet street with many small houses and apartment buildings. Tom looks at the numbers on the doors. Number ten, number twelve, number fourteen. "Ah, here it is! Number sixteen," Tom says happily.

Strona 5

Tom takes a small silver key from his pocket. He opens the big front door. Inside, it is clean and warm. He walks up the stairs to the second floor. His apartment is number 2B. He opens the door and walks inside his new home.

The apartment is small but very nice. There is a big window in the living room. The sun shines through the window. There is a comfortable grey sofa, a small television, and a white table with two chairs. Tom puts his suitcase on the floor.

Strona 6

Tom walks into the kitchen. The kitchen has a fridge, a cooker, and a small sink. He opens the fridge, but it is empty. There is no milk, no cheese, and no juice. "I need to go to the supermarket," Tom thinks. He is tired, but he needs food for dinner.

He goes to the bedroom. There is a big bed with white pillows and a large wardrobe for his clothes. He looks out the bedroom window. He can see a beautiful park with flowers and a small lake. "I like this place," Tom says and smiles.

Strona 7

Chapter 3:
Shopping for Food

Tom takes his wallet and leaves the apartment. He walks back to the big supermarket on the corner. Inside the supermarket, it is very big and there are many people. Tom takes a small shopping basket. He walks to the fruit section.

He takes four red apples and five yellow bananas. He puts them in his basket. Next, he goes to the dairy section. He needs milk for his breakfast. He takes a bottle of milk, a packet of yellow cheese, and some butter.

Strona 8

Tom walks down the bread aisle. The smell of fresh bread is wonderful. He chooses a long brown loaf of bread. He also wants something for dinner tonight. He decides to buy some pasta and a bottle of red tomato sauce. "Pasta is easy to cook," he thinks.

Tom goes to the checkout. A nice woman counts his items. "That is fifteen pounds, please," she says. Tom gives her the money. She puts the food into a big paper bag. "Thank you. Have a nice day!" she says. Tom says thank you and takes his bag.

Strona 9

When Tom walks outside, the sky is grey now. It is not sunny anymore. "Oh no, I think it is going to rain," Tom says. He walks fast. Small drops of water start to fall from the sky. He runs the last few meters to his apartment building.

He gets inside just in time. Outside, the rain is heavy now. Tom goes up to his apartment. He is safe and dry. He puts the food on the kitchen table. He takes a big glass and drinks some milk. It tastes good and fresh.

Strona 10

Chapter 4:
A New Friend

The next morning, the sun is shining again. The birds are singing in the trees outside. Tom wakes up at seven o'clock. He feels great. He goes to the kitchen and makes a cup of coffee. He eats some bread with cheese and an apple.

Today is Saturday. Tom does not have work today. He wants to explore the beautiful park near his house. He puts on his blue jeans, a white t-shirt, and his comfortable running shoes. He grabs his keys and goes downstairs.

Strona 11

The park is very close. Tom crosses the street and enters through a big black gate. The park is full of green grass and colorful flowers. There are red roses and yellow tulips. Children are playing with a ball, and people are walking happily.

Tom walks near the small lake. He sees some white ducks swimming in the water. He sits down on a green wooden bench to watch them. Suddenly, a small white football rolls to his feet. A little boy runs towards him to get the ball.

Strona 12

Tom picks up the ball and gives it to the boy with a smile. "Here you go," Tom says. "Thank you!" the boy says and runs back to his friends. A woman walks to the bench. She is the boy's mother. "Thank you," she says to Tom. "My son loves football."

"You are welcome," Tom answers. "I am Tom. I am new in this city." The woman smiles warmly. "Nice to meet you, Tom. My name is Anna. Welcome to our city! It is a beautiful place to live, and the people here are very kind."

Strona 13

Chapter 5:
A Conversation

Anna sits down on the bench. She is very friendly. "Where are you from, Tom?" she asks. "I am from a small town near the sea," Tom answers. "But I have a new job here in the city. I start my work on Monday morning."

"Oh, that is wonderful! What is your job?" Anna asks. "I am a computer teacher," Tom says. "I teach children how to use computers and how to write simple programs. I love my job because children learn very fast."

Strona 14

Anna looks surprised and happy. "Really? That is amazing! My son Leo needs a computer teacher. He loves games and wants to learn more about computers. Do you have time for private lessons on the weekend?" she asks.

Tom thinks for a moment. He wants to help and make some new friends. "Yes, I can do that," Tom says. "I am free on Saturday mornings." Anna is very glad. She takes a piece of paper and writes her telephone number on it.

Strona 15

"Here is my number," Anna says, giving the paper to Tom. "Call me next week, and we can talk about the details. Now I must go. Leo is hungry, and we need to have lunch. Goodbye, Tom! It was very nice to meet you today."

"Goodbye, Anna. Nice to meet you too," Tom says. He watches Anna and Leo walk away. Tom feels very happy. He has a new friend and a student already. The city is a very good place for him. He stands up and continues his walk.

Strona 16

Chapter 6:
Exploring the Town

Tom walks out of the park. He wants to see more of the city center. He walks for twenty minutes and arrives at a big square. In the middle of the square, there is a large, old fountain with beautiful stone statues. Water is splashing everywhere.

Around the square, there are many interesting shops. There is a big bookstore, a clothing store, a music shop, and a cinema. Tom loves books, so he walks into the bookstore. The shop is quiet and smells like old paper and tea.

Strona 17

There are thousands of books on the wooden shelves. Tom looks at the history section, then the fiction section. He finds an interesting book about the history of the city. The book has many old black and white photographs of the streets.

Tom takes the book to the counter. A man with big glasses stands there. "This is a very good book," the man says. "It tells the story of our city from one hundred years ago." Tom pays for the book. He wants to read it at home tonight.

Strona 18

Tom leaves the bookstore and looks at his watch. It is four o'clock in the afternoon. He feels a little tired from all the walking. He decides to take a bus back to his apartment. He sees a red bus stop near the square.

He waits for five minutes. Bus number twelve arrives. Tom gets on the bus and buys a ticket from the driver. He sits near the window. The bus moves slowly through the traffic. Tom looks at the shops and houses as they pass by.

Strona 19

Chapter 7:
A Quiet Evening

Tom arrives at his apartment building. He walks up the stairs and opens his door. The apartment is quiet and peaceful. He puts his new book on the table. He changes his clothes into comfortable grey sweatpants and a warm sweater.

He goes to the kitchen to cook dinner. Tonight he is making the pasta he bought yesterday. He boils some water in a pot, adds the pasta, and heats the tomato sauce. The dinner smells very good. He puts the pasta on a white plate.

Strona 20

Tom sits at the kitchen table and eats his dinner. The pasta is hot and delicious. After dinner, he washes his plate and fork in the sink. He likes to keep his kitchen clean. Then, he makes a cup of sweet chamomile tea.

He sits on the grey sofa with his tea and his new history book. He opens the first page. The photographs are beautiful. He reads about the old trains and the first houses in the city. The city has a very interesting story.

Strona 21

At ten o'clock, Tom feels very sleepy. He closes the book and turns off the television. He checks the door to see if it is locked. Then he goes to the bedroom. The bed is soft and comfortable. He falls asleep very quickly.

He dreams about the park, the ducks, and his new job. He is excited about the future. Tomorrow is Sunday, another day to rest before his big week begins. The night is quiet and dark outside his window.

Strona 22

Chapter 8:
Sunday Plans

Tom wakes up early on Sunday morning. The sun is not fully up yet, and the sky is a soft pink color. Tom likes mornings because they are very calm. He does some light exercises in the living room to stretch his body.

He makes breakfast: two fried eggs, some toast with butter, and a glass of orange juice. While eating, he thinks about his clothes for Monday. "I need to look professional for my first day at the new school," Tom says to himself.

Strona 23

He opens his large wardrobe in the bedroom. He chooses a nice blue shirt and dark grey trousers. He looks at the shirt. It has some wrinkles. "I need to iron this shirt," Tom thinks. He finds an iron in the utility closet.

Tom irons his shirt carefully. Now it looks perfect. He hangs it up in the wardrobe. He also cleans his black leather shoes until they shine. Now his clothes are ready for Monday morning. Tom feels prepared and confident.

Strona 24

In the afternoon, Tom goes for a short walk around his neighborhood. He wants to find the best way to get to the school. The school is not very far. It is only a fifteen-minute walk from his apartment building.

He finds the school easily. It is a big modern building with large windows and a big playground for the kids. There is a sign that says "Green Valley Primary School". Tom stands outside and looks at it. "This is where I work now," he thinks.

Strona 25

Chapter 9:
The First Day

Monday morning arrives. Tom's alarm clock rings at six o'clock. Tom jumps out of bed. He is excited and a little nervous. He takes a hot shower, shaves, and puts on his clean blue shirt and grey trousers. He looks good.

He eats a quick breakfast of cereal and milk. He puts his laptop, a notebook, and a blue pen into his black bag. He locks his apartment door and walks down the stairs. The morning air is cool and fresh.

Strona 26

Tom walks to the school. He sees many children walking with their parents. The children have colorful school bags on their backs. They are laughing and talking. Tom enters the school through the main glass doors.

He goes to the main office. A pleasant woman is sitting behind a desk. "Hello," Tom says. "My name is Tom Harrison. I am the new computer teacher." The woman smiles. "Welcome, Mr. Harrison! I am Sarah, the school secretary."

Strona 27

Sarah gives Tom a plastic badge with his name on it and a set of keys. "Your computer room is room number twenty-four on the second floor," she explains. "The principal, Mr. Davis, wants to see you at nine o'clock in his office."

Tom thanks Sarah and walks to the second floor. He finds room twenty-four and opens the door with his key. The room is big and bright. There are twenty modern computers on clean white desks. There is also a big whiteboard on the wall.

Strona 28

Chapter 10:
Meeting the Principal

Tom turns on the main computer to check the system. Everything works perfectly. The internet is fast, and the screens are clean. At five minutes to nine, Tom walks down to the principal's office on the first floor.

He knocks on the door. "Come in," a strong voice says. Tom enters the room. Mr. Davis is an older man with grey hair and a friendly face. He is wearing a dark suit. He stands up and shakes Tom's hand warmly.

Strona 29

"Good morning, Mr. Harrison," Mr. Davis says. "Welcome to Green Valley School. We are very happy to have you here. Our students want to learn about computers, and I know you are an excellent teacher."

"Thank you, Mr. Davis," Tom says. "I am very happy to be here. The computer room looks wonderful, and I have many interesting lessons ready for the children." Mr. Davis smiles and nods his head in approval.

Strona 30

"Excellent," Mr. Davis says. "Your first class starts at ten o'clock. They are the youngest students, seven years old. Keep it simple and fun today." Tom agrees. He takes his schedule and leaves the principal's office.

He goes back to room twenty-four. He sits at his desk and prepares his presentation. He wants to show the children the basic parts of a computer: the monitor, the mouse, the keyboard, and the main computer box.

Strona 31

Chapter 11:
The First Lesson

At ten o'clock, the door opens. A group of young children walks into the room. They look curious and excited. They look at the computers and then at Tom. "Good morning, everyone!" Tom says with a loud, friendly voice.

"Good morning!" the children reply together. Tom tells them to sit down at the desks. One boy and one girl sit at each computer. Tom stands near the whiteboard. "My name is Mr. Harrison, and I am your new teacher," he says.

Strona 32

Tom points to his screen. "Today, we are going to learn about the computer mouse," Tom says. "The mouse is like your digital hand. When you move the mouse, the little arrow on the screen moves too. Let us try it together."

The children look at their mice. They start to move them around. Some children laugh as the arrows dance on the screens. Tom walks around the room to help a small girl who is having trouble. "Hold it gently like this," Tom shows her.

Strona 33

Next, Tom shows them a simple drawing game on the computer. The children use the mouse to paint pictures of houses, animals, and suns. They enjoy the game very much. The classroom is full of happy sounds and clicking noises.

The lesson finishes at eleven o'clock. "Time to go, children," Tom says. "Please turn off your screens." The children stand up. "Thank you, Mr. Harrison! The lesson was great!" a little boy says. Tom smiles. "See you next week!"

Strona 34

Chapter 12:
Lunch and a Call

Tom has a break for one hour. He goes to the staff room on the first floor. There is a small kitchen with a coffee machine and a microwave. Other teachers are sitting on comfortable chairs, drinking tea and talking about their classes.

Tom meets Linda, the art teacher, and James, the maths teacher. They are very welcoming. Tom eats his chicken and rice lunch that he brought from home. He enjoys talking to his new colleagues. They give him helpful tips about the school.

Strona 35

After lunch, Tom remembers the paper in his wallet. It is Anna's phone number. He takes his phone out and decides to call her. The phone rings three times before Anna answers. "Hello?" her voice says over the phone.

"Hello, Anna. This is Tom, the computer teacher from the park," Tom says. "Oh, hello Tom! How are you? How is your first day at work?" Anna asks kindly. "It is going very well, thank you. My first class was wonderful," Tom replies.

Strona 36

"That is great to hear," Anna says. "Are you still available to teach Leo this Saturday?" Tom answers, "Yes, absolutely. Is ten o'clock in the morning okay for you?" Anna says that ten o'clock is perfect for them.

She gives Tom her home address. She lives on Bluebird Lane, which is very close to Tom's apartment. "Thank you, Tom. Leo is very excited about the class," Anna says. "You are welcome. See you on Saturday," Tom says and hangs up.

Strona 37

Chapter 13:
A Good Start

Tom teaches two more classes in the afternoon. The older students learn about typing on the keyboard. They practice typing their names and short sentences. Tom is patient and helps everyone who makes mistakes.

At half past three, the school day ends. The children leave the building, and the school becomes very quiet. Tom cleans his room, turns off all the computers, and locks the door. He walks down to the main office to return the keys.

Strona 38

"Have a good evening, Mr. Harrison," Sarah says from behind her desk. "Thank you, Sarah. You too," Tom says. He walks out of the school. The afternoon sun is warm, and he feels very satisfied with his first day.

He walks back to his apartment slowly. He thinks about his life in this new city. Only a few days ago, he was alone and knew nobody. Now, he has an apartment, a great job, friendly colleagues, and new friends in the park.

Strona 39

He arrives at his building and checks his mailbox. There is a small letter inside. It is a welcome card from the building manager. "Welcome to Green Street! We hope you love your apartment," the note says. Tom smiles at the kind message.

He goes up to his room and puts his bag down. He feels at home now. He opens the window to let the fresh air inside. He hears the soft sounds of the city and the distant laughter of children playing in the park across the street.

Strona 40

Chapter 14:
Happy Future

Tom knows that his new life is just beginning. There will be busy days and quiet days, but he is ready for everything. He feels lucky to be a teacher and to help people learn every day. He looks forward to his weekend lesson with Leo.

He sits down at his white table with a glass of cold orange juice. He looks at his history book and then out at the beautiful green park. "This is a good city," Tom whispers to himself with a peaceful smile. "This is my home now."
